The Federation

Inter-house rails for colive.fun. One house solves coordination for eight people. The problems left over — idle tools, retail prices, labor gluts, rent shocks, where a split lands — are exactly the ones no single house can solve alone.

A working single-neighborhood prototype (tool library + bulk orders) was built and shelved for this design pass · ←/→ to move

Why

Every commune movement that survived, federated

Hutterites

The leut

Colonies of ~100 cap their size and split — but the leut (federation) finances the daughter colony, shares combine harvesters, and acts as the insurance pool. The split protocol we shipped assumes this layer exists.

Kibbutzim

Regional co-ops

Individual kibbutzim were small; their purchasing federations and regional factories got them industrial-scale prices and credit no single settlement could touch.

Rochdale → CWS

Wholesale society

Retail co-ops federated into the Co-operative Wholesale Society within 19 years — because member stores couldn't beat wholesale prices alone. Bulk buying is the oldest federation move there is.

Mondragon

Inter-co-op funds

Worker co-ops pool a share of surplus into common funds that absorb shocks and capitalize new co-ops. Solidarity as a balance sheet line, not a vibe.

The pattern: sovereignty at the small unit, shared capital and shared risk at the federation.

The design principle

Subsidiarity, enforced by architecture

The house stays sovereign. The federation only holds what a single house cannot: shared capital goods, shared price power, shared risk. Nothing moves up a level without a vote at that level.

Stays in the house
  • Ledger, chores, meals, bounties
  • The agreement & its votes
  • All member data — houses are opaque to each other by default
Goes to the federation
  • Tool registry & loan escrows
  • Bulk-order pledges
  • Solidarity fund & claims
  • Cross-house labor clearing
Exists nowhere
  • No token, no reputation score
  • No global feed of house internals
  • No custody — same rule as gathering deposits

Ostrom's eighth design principle — nested enterprises — is the whole slide in four words.

Layer 1 · prototyped

The tool library

A power drill is used for 13 minutes in its lifetime. Six houses do not need six dehydrators, five ladders, and two pickup trucks.

Prototype (built, shelved)

Borrow / return

Registry of tools with owner house, availability, 14-day due dates; borrowing and returning drove the house ledger. Worked end-to-end in the local-first store.

Federation version

Deposit-backed loans

Borrowing house escrows a deposit against the tool (the GatheringEscrow pattern, verbatim). Returned on time → refund. Late or lost → owner claims. Free-riding priced, not policed.

The honest cost

Logistics, not code

Pickup and drop-off friction kills tool libraries more than defection does. Anchor each tool to a host house with stated hours; the truck is itself a shared tool.

Layer 2 · prototyped

Bulk orders — threshold pledges

🌾 Any house opens an order→ Houses pledge quantities→ Threshold met → locked→ Each share lands on that house's ledger
Prototype (built, shelved)

Open orders with unit prices and thresholds; pledges tracked per house; locking wrote my house's share straight onto the shared-expense ledger as a groceries entry, split across members. All local-first.

Federation version

A pledge is an escrow deposit in xDai — the order locking releases funds to the coordinating house, which rotates monthly like a bill. Under-threshold orders auto-refund on expiry. No one fronts money for neighbors; the contract does.

Wholesale case rates run 20–40% under retail on staples — the discount is the recruitment pitch for house #7.

Layer 3

Hours clear across houses

The labor-credit ledger already counts hours inside a house. Federation means Marcus fixing Bushwick Static's boiler creates a 3-hour credit that Cypress can spend on Bushwick's welder next month.

House-to-house, not person-to-person

Debts net between houses, like the ledger's simplify-debts. People stay inside their house's books; the federation sees only house balances.

Anchored, not floating

Pure time-banks decay when hours have no floor. Each house sets its rate (the Points & Pool knob, already shipped); imbalances settle in hours first, xDai as the release valve.

Skill discovery for free

The federation skill directory is the quiz's values/skills fields, aggregated house-level and opt-in. No new data collected.

Layer 4 · the serious one

The solidarity fund

Rent shock, job loss, a boiler that dies in January. Barn-raising, as a balance sheet.

Mechanics
  • Monthly premium per house (say $20/member) into a pool contract on Gnosis
  • Claims filed by a house, not a person
  • Released by federation vote — the same 2/3 machinery, one house one vote
Failure modes, priced in
  • Adverse selection: premiums scale with claims history per house
  • Moral hazard: claims are loans first, grants by supermajority
  • Capture: pool capped at N months of premiums; overflow rebates
Why chain

The pool is the first place real custody could creep in. A minimal contract — deposits in, 2/3-of-houses signature out, everything public — keeps the platform's hands off the money, same promise as gathering deposits. Explicitly not a Safe multisig UI; one small purpose-built contract.

Layer 5

Where daughter houses land

The shipped split protocol creates a daughter house with members, a fund share, and no lease. The Hutterite leut solves this by provisioning the daughter colony before the split. Ours:

Lead pipeline

Federation-level list of viable leases and landlords who've already rented to a member house — the highest-value shared asset in NYC.

Guarantor capacity

The solidarity fund co-signs or fronts a deposit as a structured loan, repaid from the daughter's pool over 12 months.

Furniture day

Tool library + truck + a federation work-day gathering (the gathering flow already handles this, escrow and all).

Governance

One house, one vote — and no federation class

Inside the house

Shipped

2/3 pass-the-phone votes run spending, disputes, and agreement amendments today. Unchanged.

Between houses

Delegates, rotating

Each house's delegate seat rotates like a bill rotation — deterministic, no elections, no permanent federation people. Ostrom again: those affected by the rules participate in changing them.

Deliberately missing

No federation veto over houses

The federation can eject a house from shared pools (2/3), but can never reach inside one — no lever exists to pull.

Architecture

Local-first houses, chain-shared federation

The unsolved problem in the current app: two houses' browsers share no state. Three candidate transports:

TransportHowVerdict
Chain-onlyEvery pledge/loan/claim is a Gnosis tx; UIs read contract state. Registry contract holds the house roster.Recommended — rails exist, writes cost pennies, custody stays impossible, and reads are free via the public RPC we already use.
Git-backed JSONA shared repo branch as the registry (the pattern our address manifest already uses).Fallback — fine for the tool catalog, wrong for money; merge conflicts are governance disputes in disguise.
Hosted backendA small KV/worker service.Rejected — reintroduces the server and the custodian the whole design avoids.

New contracts needed: FederationRegistry (roster + delegates), ToolEscrow and PledgePool (both ~GatheringEscrow-sized), SolidarityPool. All small, all forge-testable, all deployed by the existing etherform CI.

Restraint

What the federation will never be

No token

xDai is the unit. A federation token is a governance attack surface and a speculation magnet with zero added function.

No reputation scores

Deposits price risk; scores moralize it. The quiz taught us: measure behavior, never rank people.

No house transparency mandate

Houses publish five lens dots and what they pledge. Ledgers, members, agreements stay home.

No custody

If the platform can touch the money, the design failed. Same rule since the first gathering deposit.
